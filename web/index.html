How SeaDisco Works

Searching

Type anything into the main search box — artist, album, label, genre, or a mix — to search the entire Discogs database. Click Advanced to open targeted fields: Artist, Release Title, Label, Year, Genre & Style, and Format. Each field is sent to Discogs as its own filter, so you can combine them freely.

Use the radio buttons to narrow results by type: All · Masters · Releases · Artists · Labels. Masters group every pressing of a recording together; Releases are individual pressings by country, label, year, and format. Sort by relevance, year, title, or label — your sort is saved with each search.

Clicking a result card opens full details: tracklist with YouTube preview links and ⌕ icons to find other versions, pressing info, credits, market price, and photos. Track titles in Master popups include a format filter so you can narrow pressings to Vinyl, CD, Cassette, etc. instantly. The Hide owned checkbox (active when your collection is synced) removes items already in your collection from any search result page.

✦ AI Search

Select AI from the result type buttons to describe what you're looking for in plain language — "obscure 70s Nigerian funk", "music like early Miles Davis", "best post-war Chicago blues guitarists". Claude returns 12 curated picks with descriptions and direct links to Discogs searches. AI searches are not saved to your history. Requires a free account.

Collection, Wantlist & Wanted

Signed-in users with a Discogs token can sync their full collection and wantlist — no size limit, whether you have 500 or 15,000 records. Sync runs silently in the background with live progress and picks up automatically when you sign in if your data is more than 6 hours old.

Once synced, the Collection and Wantlist tabs become fully searchable with dedicated filters for artist, release, label, year, genre, style, and format. Genre and style filters are dropdowns built from your actual data. Filters support + (AND), | (OR), and - (exclude) operators. Green ✓ and red ♡ badges appear on matching cards across all search results.

The Wanted tab (visible once signed in) shows 500 random items from the wantlists of all SeaDisco members — interleaved fairly across users so no single collection dominates. A quick search bar filters across title, artist, label, genre, style, and year. A good way to discover what serious collectors are actually looking for.

Latest Drops

The Drops tab shows 150 randomly selected releases from a 3-month rolling pool sourced from ListenBrainz and MusicBrainz, refreshed every 6 hours. Each visit draws a fresh random 150 — so the selection changes every time. Filter instantly by genre and style using the tag cloud above the cards. The Google → link on each card searches the web for that artist and release — useful for finding it beyond Discogs.

Recent Searches & Discovery

The pill cloud below search results shows recent searches from all SeaDisco users that surfaced an artist or label bio — a signal that the search led somewhere interesting. Click any pill to instantly re-run that search. Hover to see the full text.

Your Token & Search Limits

Anonymous users share the site's Discogs token, capped at 25 searches per day. Sign up for a free account and add your own Discogs token for unlimited personal searches with no shared bottleneck. Get your token at discogs.com → Settings → Developers → Generate new token, then paste it on your Account page.
