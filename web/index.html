How SeaDisco Works

Searching

Search the entire Discogs database by artist, album, label, or any combination. Click Advanced for targeted fields (Artist, Release, Label, Year, Genre/Style, Format). Narrow by type — Masters (every pressing grouped) or Releases (individual pressings). Sort by relevance, year, title, or label.

Click any card for full details: tracklist with YouTube previews, version finder, pressing info, credits, and photos. From the detail popup you can add/remove items from your collection or wantlist and rate releases 1–5 stars. Hide owned removes synced collection items from results. Select ✦ AI to describe what you want in plain language — Claude returns 12 curated picks with Discogs links.

Latest Drops

150 random picks from a rolling 3-month database of 20,000+ releases via ListenBrainz/MusicBrainz, refreshed every 6 hours. Filter by the tag cloud or genre dropdown, or search the full database by artist, title, or tag.

Click any card to search Discogs — if matched, the full popup opens with alternatives. No match shows ListenBrainz info with clickable tags and a Google link.

Live

Search upcoming concerts and events by artist, city/zip code, or genre. Results pull from both Ticketmaster and Bandsintown, merged and deduped. Artist searches include a Bandsintown photo when available. Each listing links to Google for tickets. Your last searched city is saved for convenience.

Feed

Music news, reviews, and videos from Pitchfork, Bandcamp Daily, Stereogum, The Quietus, Aquarium Drunkard, and select YouTube channels. Updated every 4 hours. Filter by category — news, reviews, gear, or video — or search by keyword.

Vinyl

Browse live eBay auctions for 12″ vinyl LPs, updated hourly. Filter by price tier, sort by ending soonest, most bids, or price. Each card shows current bid, bid count, and time remaining — click through to bid on eBay.

Gear

Browse live eBay auctions for vintage hi-fi and audio equipment, updated hourly. Filter by price tier, sort by ending soonest, most bids, or price. Each card shows current bid, bid count, and time remaining — click through to bid on eBay.

My Records

Connect your Discogs account to sync your full collection, wantlist, inventory, and lists — any size. You can connect via OAuth (one-click authorization) or by pasting a Personal Access Token. Sync runs in the background and refreshes automatically each night.

The Collection and Wantlist tabs have dedicated filters (artist, release, label, year, genre, style, format) with + (AND), | (OR), and - (exclude) operators. Green ✓ and red ♡ badges mark owned and wanted items across all results.

The Inventory tab shows your marketplace listings, and Lists displays your curated Discogs lists.

Collection Actions

From any release detail popup, you can add or remove items from your collection and wantlist with one click. Rate releases 1–5 stars — ratings sync to your Discogs account. All actions update instantly with optimistic UI and revert gracefully if anything goes wrong.

Account & Limits

Anonymous users are capped at 5 searches/day on a shared token. Sign up free and connect your Discogs account — either via OAuth or by adding your own token (discogs.com → Settings → Developers) — for unlimited searches and full features.

Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 30, 2026

What We Collect

When you create an account, we store your email address (provided by our authentication provider, Clerk) and any Discogs credentials you choose to connect — either a Personal Access Token or OAuth access credentials.

When you sync your collection, we store a local copy of your Discogs collection, wantlist, inventory, and list metadata so you can browse and search them on SeaDisco. We also cache your Discogs profile information (username, avatar, and account stats) for display on the Account page.

We log API usage for operational monitoring. This data is used solely for debugging and improving the service. We do not store your search queries, location data, or taste profiles.

Third-Party Services

SeaDisco integrates with the following third-party services:

  • Discogs — music database searches, collection syncing, and collection actions, using your personal API token or OAuth authorization
  • Clerk — authentication and account management
  • Ticketmaster & Bandsintown — live event data (no personal data is shared)
  • eBay — vintage audio gear and vinyl record listings (no personal data is shared)
  • ListenBrainz / MusicBrainz — fresh release data (no personal data is shared)
  • YouTube — music video embeds in the feed (standard YouTube embed; subject to Google's privacy policy)
  • Google Analytics — anonymous usage statistics to help us improve the site

OAuth Authorization

If you connect via OAuth, SeaDisco receives limited access to your Discogs account through the standard OAuth 1.0a flow. We store the resulting access token and secret to make API requests on your behalf. We do not receive or store your Discogs password. You can disconnect OAuth at any time from the Account page, which immediately revokes our access.

How We Use Your Data

Your Discogs credentials (token or OAuth) are used solely to make API requests to Discogs on your behalf — searching, syncing your collection, and managing your collection/wantlist. We never share your credentials with any other service or third party. Your collection data is used only to display your records within SeaDisco.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data with anyone.

Data Storage & Security

All data is stored in a secured PostgreSQL database hosted on Railway. Your Discogs credentials are stored encrypted at rest by the database provider. OAuth secrets are stored server-side only and are never exposed to the browser. Access to the database is restricted to the application server only.

Data Retention & Deletion

User data (collection, wantlist, inventory, lists, and orders) is automatically purged after 30 days or deleted by the user. Your authentication tokens are retained for as long as your account exists.

Temporary operational data is also pruned automatically: expired gear listings, past live events, API logs, and feed article caches.

You can delete your account at any time from the Account tab. Deleting your account permanently removes all of your data from our database, including your email, Discogs credentials, collection, wantlist, inventory, lists, orders, and profile data. Your Clerk authentication account is also deleted. This action is immediate and irreversible — there is no way to recover your data after deletion.

Cookies

SeaDisco uses cookies only for authentication (via Clerk) and anonymous analytics (via Google Analytics). We do not use advertising or tracking cookies, and we do not sell any data.
