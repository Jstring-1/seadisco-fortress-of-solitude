How SeaDisco Works

Basic Search

Type anything into the main search box — artist name, album title, label, genre, or a mix. SeaDisco searches the entire Discogs database of over 15 million releases.

Advanced Search

Click Advanced to unlock targeted fields: Artist, Release Title, Label, Year, Genre & Style, and Format. Advanced fields are only applied when the panel is open.

Result Types

Filter by All · Masters · Releases · Artists · Labels. Masters group all pressings of a recording; Releases are specific pressings by country, year, and format.

✦ AI Search

Select AI to ask Claude for music recommendations in plain language — "obscure 70s Nigerian funk", "music like early Miles Davis", "best post-war Chicago blues guitarists". Returns 6–8 curated picks linking to Discogs searches. Requires a free account.

Result Quality Phrase

After each search, Claude briefly evaluates the results and appends a short honest phrase to the result count — e.g. "Exact artist found, mostly albums". This is a best-effort assessment and may occasionally be off.

Sort

Sort by relevance, year, title, or label. The selected sort is saved with your search and restored when you click a recent search pill.

Result Cards

Click any card to see full release details — tracklist, credits, pressing info, market price, and photos. Click the cover art to browse all images. Artist and label names link to their Discogs pages within SeaDisco.

Latest Drops

The Drops tab shows new releases sourced from ListenBrainz and MusicBrainz, refreshed every 6 hours and kept for 4 weeks. Filter by genre/style tags using the tag cloud at the top. The Search → link on each card opens a Google search for that artist and release so you can find it anywhere on the web — not just Discogs.

Recent Searches

The pill cloud below search results shows recent searches from all SeaDisco users. Click any pill to instantly re-run that search, including its sort. Hover a pill to see the full untruncated search text.

Sharing

Every search and every page (Drops, Info, Collection, Wantlist) lives in the URL. Copy it from your browser to share exact results with anyone — no account needed to view.

My Collection & Wantlist

Signed-in users with a Discogs token can browse their collection and wantlist directly from SeaDisco. Records sync from Discogs (up to 1,000 at a time) and are cached locally for fast browsing. Green ✓ and red ♡ badges appear on matching search results.

Search Rate & Your Token

SeaDisco runs on the site owner's Discogs API token, which is shared across all anonymous users and capped at 1 search per second by Discogs. Anonymous users also get 5 free searches per day before hitting the limit. Create a free account and add your own Discogs token to get unlimited personal searches with no shared bottleneck. Get your token at discogs.com → Settings → Developers → Generate new token, then paste it in your Account page.
